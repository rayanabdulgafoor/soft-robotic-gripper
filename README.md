## Overview

The Soft Robotic Gripper project focused on designing and fabricating a flexible pneumatic gripper using molding and casting techniques. The project explored soft robotics concepts by developing a silicone-based actuator capable of controlled bending motion through selective material expansion.
![Hero Image](/assets/sft_hero_shot03.jpg)
A three-part mold was designed in Fusion 360 and fabricated using a PRUSA i3 MK3S 3D printer. Silicone casting was used to create the actuator body, while fabric reinforcement was integrated to restrict expansion on one side of the actuator, enabling controlled directional movement when pressurized air was applied.

## Mold Design

The mold system was designed in Fusion 360 and consisted of:

* Upper mold
* Middle mold
* Bottom mold

![Hero Image](/assets/cured_silicone_with_mold_pic_01.jpg)

The upper and middle molds were used for casting the top actuator section, while the bottom mold was used for the lower actuator layer.

The mold geometry was designed to:

* define internal air channels,
* support controlled deformation,
* and simplify demolding during fabrication.

---

## Fabrication Workflow

### 3D Printing

The mold components were fabricated using a PRUSA i3 MK3S 3D printer.

Post-processing included:

* support material removal,
* surface cleaning,
* and heat-assisted finishing.

### Silicone Casting

The actuator was cast using:

* Aditya Silicone Rubber
* Aditya Curing Agent

Mixing ratio:

```text id="jlwm111"
100 : 3
```

![Hero Image](/assets/gripper_silicone_casting_pic_04.jpg)

The silicone mixture was prepared and degassed using a vacuum chamber to remove trapped air bubbles before casting.

---

## Fabric Reinforcement

Fabric reinforcement was integrated between the silicone layers to restrict expansion on one side of the actuator.

![Hero Image](/assets/fabric_cuting_in_zund_pic_001.jpg)

The reinforcement fabric was:

* designed in Fusion 360,
* exported as DXF,
* and precision-cut using a Zund Digital Cutter.


![Hero Image](/assets/fabric_cuting_in_zund_pic_002.jpg)
This reinforcement mechanism ensured:

* directional bending,
* controlled deformation,
* and improved actuator response.

---

## Vacuum Chamber Process

A vacuum chamber was used during casting to:

* remove trapped air bubbles,
* improve casting consistency,
* reduce internal voids,
* and improve overall actuator quality.

![Hero Image](/assets/vc_gripper_silicone_casting_pic_05.jpg)

![Hero Image](/assets/vc_gripper_silicone_casting_pic_08.png)

The degassing process significantly improved the reliability and repeatability of the final soft actuator.
